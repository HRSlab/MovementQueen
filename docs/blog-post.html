Why 'Stretching' Isn't The Answer For Your Tight Hips

Posted on June 10, 2025 by Tina Davis

Most of us have been taught to stretch when we feel tightness. It's an almost instinctual reaction. But what if that's not the best solution for chronic tightness, especially in our hips? What if the feeling of "tightness" isn't a problem with the muscle itself, but a signal from our nervous system?

In my practice, we explore the idea that chronic muscle tension is often a protective response. Your brain perceives a lack of stability or control in a certain range of motion, and in response, it "puts on the brakes" by creating tension. This is a brilliant safety mechanism, but endlessly stretching into that tension can sometimes feel like fighting against your own body's wisdom.

The Neurological Perspective

Instead of thinking of muscles as simple rubber bands, we view them as being under the constant control of your nervous system. From this perspective, creating lasting mobility isn't about forcing a muscle to lengthen; it's about convincing your nervous system that it's safe to allow more movement.

  • Active Mobility Drills: Instead of passive holds, we use active movements to teach your nervous system to control new ranges of motion.
  • Strengthening at End-Ranges: By building strength in the very positions where you feel "tight," you prove to your brain that you are safe and stable there.
  • Mindful Body Awareness: We focus on feeling and understanding the signals your body is sending, rather than just trying to silence them with stretching.

The next time you feel that familiar tightness in your hips, I invite you to pause. Instead of immediately pulling on it, ask yourself: what is my body trying to tell me? Perhaps the answer isn't more flexibility, but more strength, more control, and more mindful awareness.
